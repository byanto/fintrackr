# 🚀 Getting Back Into Coding After Years Away – My Comeback Plan

Hi there! 👋

This post marks the beginning of my journey back into professional software development. After years away, I’ve decided to rebuild my technical skills and re-enter the field—starting with this project: **Fintrackr**.

---

## 👨‍💻 My Background

I hold a **Master’s degree in Computer Science from TU Berlin** (graduated in 2016), and also completed my Bachelor's degree at TU Berlin. During my time in Germany, I had the opportunity to work as a **Werkstudent at TU Berlin's Informatik Rechnerbetrieb (IRB)** and later at **KNIME**, where i contributed as a Software Developer intern. I also spent two years as an exchange student at **Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU)**, which gave me valuable international experience.

---

## ⏳ My Gap Years (2016–2025)

Since 2016, I’ve been based in Indonesia. Though I stayed active in tech-related education and business, I wasn’t working as a full-time programmer. I ran successful e-commerce businesses on various marketplaces in Indonesia. e.g. Shopee, Tokopedia, and Lazada. I also occasionally taught Computer Science for A-Level students as a freelancer. Now, I’m making a return—stronger and more motivated than ever.

---

## 🌟 Why Now?

I’ve always loved coding and problem-solving. This year (2025), I made a clear decision: **I want to return to Germany as a backend developer by late 2025 or early 2026**.

I still have solid foundations in **Python and Java**, and I’ve chosen to **focus on Java + Spring Boot** to align with the backend job market in Germany.

## 📦 The Project: Fintrackr

I’m passionate about personal finance and stock investing — and I saw an opportunity to build a project that reflects this. **Fintrackr** is my hands-on learning project—a **personal finance tracker** built with Spring Boot 3 and Java 21.

Goals:
- Practice **clean architecture** and **TDD**
- Build **RESTful APIs**
- Apply **CI/CD**, **JaCoCo coverage**, and **Dockerization**
- Learn GitHub Actions and code quality tools

This project will evolve as I explore more features like authentication, transaction categories, and investment monitoring.

You can check it out here: [https://github.com/byanto/fintrackr](https://github.com/byanto/fintrackr)
